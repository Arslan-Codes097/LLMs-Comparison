# Llama 3.3 70B Versatile (via Groq) Summary

Researchers at NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory have determined that a near-Earth object, provisionally known as the asteroid 1998 SH2, is actually a comet. The object, which passed within 2 million miles of Earth on August 28, 2025, had been tracked since its discovery in 1998, but its motion was found to be irregular, suggesting it might be a comet. By using optical astrometry and powerful observatories, including the Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope and the European Southern Observatory's Very Large Telescope, scientists detected a weak but clear tail, confirming that 1998 SH2 is a comet, and it will receive an additional comet provisional designation, P/1998 SH2. This discovery has implications for planetary defense and the study of "dark comets," a class of objects that exhibit irregularities in their trajectory but lack visible evidence of comet activity.
