# DeepSeek Summary

A NASA JPL study, published July 16, 2026, in Nature Astronomy, found that the object provisionally known as asteroid 1998 SH2 is actually a comet. On August 28, 2025, it passed within 2 million miles (3 million km) of Earth during its 4½-year orbit. When NASA's Deep Space Network radar didn't find it at the expected position, lead researcher Davide Farnocchia measured nongravitational perturbations, suggesting gas venting from solar heating. Observations from the 3.6-meter Canada-France-Hawaii Telescope, the 1.5-meter Danish Telescope, and the 8.2-meter Very Large Telescope confirmed a weak tail, verifying the hypothesis, as coauthor Olivier Hainaut stated. The object now receives comet designation P/1998 SH2. The research aids planetary defense by distinguishing comets from asteroids, as outgassing perturbs comet motion more, affecting Earth impact risks, and relates to dark comets. NASA's upcoming NEO Surveyor will support this effort.
