# Gemini Summary

A July 16, 2026, release regarding a study led by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory and published in Nature Astronomy reveals that near-Earth object 1998 SH2 is a comet rather than an asteroid. On August 28, 2025, the object safely passed within 2 million miles (3 million kilometers) of Earth during its 42-year orbit. When researchers attempted to observe it using NASA's Deep Space Network, it was missing from its predicted position. Lead researcher Davide Farnocchia determined that venting gas was altering its path. Telescopes in Hawaii and Chile subsequently detected a faint tail, confirming the comet hypothesis of coauthor Olivier Hainaut. The object will receive the additional designation P/1998 SH2. These findings suggest other irregular "dark comets" may be regular comets, which NASA's upcoming Near-Earth Object (NEO) Surveyor mission will help track for planetary defense.
